# Roadbook Spec

## Main Deliverable

The main deliverable is a vertical mobile-first HTML roadbook, with optional PDF export. Markdown is an editable draft, not the main user-facing output.

## Page Count Target

For a 20-25 day Xinjiang self-driving route, plan for dozens of vertical pages. A practical target is 60-90 pages depending on complexity.

Suggested structure:

| Module | Pages | Purpose |
|---|---:|---|
| Cover | 1 | Establish magazine feeling |
| How to use | 1 | Explain how to use it before and during the trip |
| Overview | 2-4 | Route, mileage, rhythm, risks |
| Route maps | 2-3 | Overall and segment maps |
| Daily chapters | 25-50 | 1-2 pages per day, 3 for complex days |
| Core spots | 10-20 | Must-see details and tradeoffs |
| Hotels and contacts | 4-8 | Hotel candidates and phone status |
| Food and supply | 4-8 | Restaurants, local food, backup meals, fuel/charging |
| Weather and clothing | 3-5 | Region and scenario clothing guidance |
| Risks and backups | 4-8 | Road, altitude, fatigue, weather, booking failure |
| Checklist and budget | 3-7 | Departure checks, verification, budget |

## Daily Chapter Minimum

Each day must answer:

- start and end;
- distance and drive time;
- suggested departure time and latest arrival time;
- daily intensity;
- route and key roads;
- food plan, especially lunch around 11:00;
- afternoon decision point around 16:00;
- hotel, phone status, address, parking, breakfast, laundry when available;
- must-go, optional, and not-recommended spots;
- weather and clothing;
- risks and backup plan;
- tomorrow preview.

## Operational Reading Moments

Design content around actual road use:

| Moment | User question | Roadbook answer |
|---|---|---|
| Previous night | What must be checked for tomorrow? | weather, road status, tickets, hotel, departure time |
| Morning | Where do we go and what do we wear? | summary, route, clothing, fuel/charge reminder |
| 10:30 | How long can we stay? | spot stay time and deletion trigger |
| 11:00 | Where should we eat lunch? | restaurant/area, parking, backup food |
| 14:30 | Should we still visit the afternoon spot? | latest departure and deletion rule |
| 16:00 | How far to tonight's hotel? | remaining distance, ETA, hotel phone/address/parking |
| Evening | What about dinner and tomorrow? | dinner, supply, laundry, next-day checks |
| Emergency | What if blocked/tired/sick? | conservative and emergency options |

## Daily Page Split

Use one page for normal days. Use two or three pages for complex days.

Execution page:

- title and day role;
- summary metrics;
- route map or route note;
- timeline;
- lunch, dinner, hotel;
- risk reminders.

Expansion page:

- spot details;
- best time and stay duration;
- photo/parking/ticket/toilet notes;
- optional and not-recommended spots;
- weather backup;
- hotel details.

## Food Standard

Do not write only local food names. Include:

- recommended lunch city or road segment;
- whether to enter town or eat a quick road meal;
- 2-3 restaurant candidates when available;
- dish suggestions;
- dietary alternatives;
- parking convenience;
- queue risk;
- car backup food for long driving days.

## Hotel Standard

Each night should include:

- city or area;
- why this area;
- primary hotel and 1-2 backups;
- phone, address, parking, breakfast, laundry, cancellation if available;
- verification status;
- latest arrival recommendation;
- fallback city if arriving too late.

## Spot Decision Standard

Classify spots as:

- must-go: core value, best time, stay duration, ticket/parking notes;
- optional: only if time, weather, and energy allow;
- not recommended: explain why for this route/day;
- deletion rule: what to delete first when late, tired, blocked, or weather worsens.

## Risk Standard

For high-risk nodes, provide:

- normal plan;
- conservative plan;
- emergency plan.

Cover seasonal roads, altitude, health, fatigue, night driving, wind/rain/cold/sand, booking shortage, EV charging uncertainty, border permits, tickets, and road control.
