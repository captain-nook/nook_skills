# 标题事实边界与安全规则

Use this reference before recommending any publishable title.

## Hard No

Do not invent:

- numbers, prices, scores, rankings, token counts, model versions, dates, release status;
- company names, model names, paper names, benchmark names;
- conflict between companies or models;
- affected user groups;
- legal, medical, financial, safety, or policy consequences.

Do not turn speculation into fact:

- `可能发布` cannot become `正式发布`;
- `传言/爆料` cannot become `确认`;
- `内测材料` cannot become `全量上线`;
- `某任务表现好` cannot become `全面碾压`;
- `示例 demo` cannot become `替代人类工作`.

## Boundary Labels

Use these labels in the fact hook extraction:

- `confirmed`: supported by the article, official source, direct quote, data, or visible demo.
- `source-claimed`: claimed by the article or a cited third party, but not independently verified.
- `unclear`: unclear, inferred, speculative, or missing source support.

If a title relies on `unclear` facts, mark it as risky and avoid recommending it for publication.

## Safe Replacements

- Replace `发布` with `被曝`, `传出`, `出现`, or `内测` when status is uncertain.
- Replace `碾压` with `超过`, `领先`, or `在某项测试超过` when the comparison is narrow.
- Replace `替代` with `接管一部分`, `能做`, or `开始进入` when evidence is task-specific.
- Replace `全网炸了` with a concrete affected group if the source only shows a narrow discussion.
- Replace `刚刚` with `最新` when the event is recent but not same-day.

## Scoring Priorities

Fact fidelity beats click strength.

A title with a weaker emotional hook but accurate source boundary is better than a title that gets clicks by upgrading claims.

## Red Flags

- Title has more emotional words than factual nouns.
- Title uses two or more extreme verbs in one sentence.
- Title relies on a number not present in the source.
- Title sounds like a benchmark result but source only has an anecdote.
- Title says `人类多余了` or `职业消失` without strong evidence; use as joke only when clearly framed and supported by the article tone.
